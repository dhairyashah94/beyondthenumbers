Move Your Site to Cloudflare Pages

Netlify charges per publish and you hit the monthly ceiling. Cloudflare Pages hosts the exact same site for free and never charges for republishing — so you can post a brief every day without watching a meter.

⏱ 20–25 min, once 🧩 No coding 💻 In your browser 🆓 Free forever

Why we're moving

Your Netlify bill wasn't traffic — it was publishing. Twenty deploys used all 300 credits; your actual visitors cost half a credit. On a daily publication, 20 deploys is a low ceiling you'll hit every month.

Cloudflare Pages doesn't charge per deploy. You can republish as often as you like. Same files, same site, no meter. The only Netlify-specific piece — your email subscribe form — gets swapped to a free service called Formspree in Phase 1, which I've already wired into your files.

Phase 1 — Keep your subscribe form working

Formspree delivers subscriber emails to your inbox.

Netlify's built-in form catcher only works on Netlify. Formspree does the same job on any host — it emails you whenever someone subscribes. Your forms are already set up for it; they just need your personal form ID.

1

Create a free Formspree account

Go to formspree.io/register and sign up with the email address where you want to receive subscriber notifications. The free plan handles 50 submissions a month — plenty to start.

2

Create a new form and copy its ID

Click + New form. Name it Daily Brief Subscribers and set the notification email to yours. Click Create form.

Formspree shows an endpoint that looks like https://formspree.io/f/abcdwxyz. The part after /f/ — here abcdwxyz — is your form ID. Copy it.

What you'll seeA form dashboard with that endpoint URL near the top. The 8-character code at the end is all you need.
3

Drop the ID into your files

Every subscribe form in your site currently points at a placeholder, YOUR_FORM_ID. It appears in five files: index.html and the four brief pages.

Easiest Paste your form ID to me in chat and I'll bake it into all five files and hand you the finished package — nothing to edit by hand.

Or yourself Open each file, find YOUR_FORM_ID, and replace it with your real ID. Same code in every file, so a find-and-replace across the folder does it in one shot.

Until thenThe form still looks and behaves normally on the site — it just shows a "demo mode" confirmation instead of collecting real emails until a real ID is in place.
Phase 2 — Put the site on Cloudflare Pages

Cloudflare Pages turns your files into the live website.

Pick one of two routes. If you already created the GitHub repo from the earlier setup, use Route A — it keeps one-click publishing. If you'd rather not deal with GitHub, Route B is a plain drag-and-drop.

A

Recommended Connect your GitHub repo

This keeps the publish-by-editing workflow you set up before — only the host changes from Netlify to Cloudflare.

  • Sign up at dash.cloudflare.com/sign-up (free).
  • In the left menu open Workers & Pages → Create → Pages tab → Connect to Git.
  • Authorize Cloudflare to see your GitHub, then pick your behind-the-numbers repo.
  • On the build-settings screen, leave Framework preset as None and leave the build command empty. Set Build output directory to / (just a slash). Click Save and Deploy.
What you'll seeAbout a minute later, a live link like behind-the-numbers.pages.dev. From now on, every edit you commit on GitHub auto-publishes here — free, no deploy limit.
B

No GitHub Drag-and-drop upload

Simplest possible route. The trade-off: to publish an update you re-upload the folder (still free, unlimited).

  • Sign up at dash.cloudflare.com/sign-up.
  • Open Workers & Pages → Create → Pages tab → Upload assets.
  • Name the project behind-the-numbers, then drag in the files inside your unzipped website folder — index.html must sit at the top level, not inside a sub-folder.
  • Click Deploy site.
To publish laterOpen the project → Create deployment → drag the updated files in again. Or send me your changes and I'll hand back a ready folder.
C

Give it a name and you're done

Your free address is your-project.pages.dev — that's the link you put on LinkedIn. To set a custom domain later (e.g. behindthenumbers.com), open the project → Custom domains → Set up a domain. Optional, and the only thing that ever costs money (the domain itself).

After the switch

Two small cleanups

Once your Cloudflare link works and the form is tested, tidy up the old setup:

1
Update the link you share on LinkedIn to the new .pages.dev address.
2
In Netlify, you can delete the old site (Site configuration → Delete site) so it stops counting against you. No rush — it won't charge for sitting idle, but removing it avoids confusion.

Test the form once: subscribe with your own email on the live site and confirm the notification lands in your inbox (the first submission asks you to confirm the form in Formspree — a one-time click).

Common questions
Will I lose my existing Netlify subscribers?
Any emails already captured live in your Netlify dashboard under Forms — export them (there's a download/CSV option) before you delete the site, then add them to your list. Going forward, new ones arrive via Formspree.
Is Cloudflare Pages really free for this?
Yes. Static sites like yours get unlimited bandwidth and unlimited requests on the free plan, plus 500 builds a month if you use the GitHub route — far more than a daily brief needs. There's no per-deploy charge, which was the whole Netlify problem.
Do headlines, the date, and market data still auto-update?
Yes — those refresh on their own for every visitor regardless of host. The market ticker and live quotes come straight from TradingView; nothing about the move changes them.
Can I keep using Netlify until June 27 instead?
You can — your current site keeps serving visitors, you just can't publish new briefs until credits reset. Moving to Cloudflare removes that limit permanently, which is why it's worth doing once.
What if the form doesn't send?
Check that the real form ID replaced YOUR_FORM_ID in every file, and that you confirmed the form from Formspree's first-submission email. If it's still stuck, paste me the form's endpoint and I'll check the wiring.
