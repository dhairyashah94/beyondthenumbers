01

The backlog that rewired the story

Remaining performance obligations — contracted revenue not yet booked — is the figure that turned Oracle from a mature software name into an AI-infrastructure trade. RPO closed at $638B, up 363% year-on-year and up $85B in the quarter alone (from $553B in Q3). Management says the bulk of the increase is a small number of very large AI contracts where customers prepaid for GPU capacity — roughly $75B of the backlog is prepaid or customer-supplied AI hardware. Extraordinary forward visibility, but unusually concentrated.

02

Cloud is now half the company

For the first time, cloud is larger than the legacy license business. Full-year cloud revenue hit $34.0B (+39%), led by infrastructure at +77%, while software licensing was flat at $24.5B (−1%). The pivot from a database-license company to a cloud-infrastructure company is effectively complete — and Oracle is now competing head-on with AWS, Azure and Google for AI training and inference, leaning on a sticky enterprise base that already runs its databases and ERP.

03

The catch: cash out the door

Here's the price of the growth. Oracle threw off a record $32.0B in operating cash flow, then spent $55.7B — up 162% — almost entirely on data-center build-out. Free cash flow landed at negative $23.7B. To bridge the gap, Oracle raised $48B this year in debt and equity and expects to raise about $40B more in FY2027, including a $20B share sale. Gross margin slipped ~5 points as low-margin infrastructure scaled.

04

The bet on FY2027 and beyond

Management is asking investors to look through the burn. It reaffirmed $90B of revenue for FY2027 — 34% growth, nearly double this year's pace — and nudged non-GAAP EPS guidance up to $8.05, ahead of the ~$8.01 consensus. The longer-term frame from its analyst day: a +31% revenue CAGR and +28% EPS CAGR through FY2030. The thesis in one line — the $638B backlog converts to profitable revenue fast enough to outrun the capital it's consuming.

05

The two sides of the trade

+ The bull case

  • $638B backlog (+363%) — forward visibility no enterprise-software peer can match; roughly 5× in a year.
  • IaaS +93% — OCI is taking real AI-infrastructure share, not just riding generic cloud demand.
  • Cloud ~50% of revenue — the transformation is done; mix and growth rate keep re-rating the story.
  • Guidance raised — $90B / $8.05 EPS for FY27 beats consensus; +31% rev CAGR targeted to FY30.
  • Embedded moat — databases and ERP make the installed base expensive and slow to leave.

− The bear case

  • FCF −$23.7B — record growth is being bought with record cash outlays; CapEx +162%.
  • Financing dependence — $48B raised this year, ~$40B more next, including a $20B equity sale (dilution).
  • Margins compressing — gross margin down ~5 pts as low-margin infrastructure scales.
  • Concentration risk — much of the backlog is a handful of giant prepaid-GPU AI deals; conversion isn't guaranteed.
  • Rising leverage — combined debt and lease obligations ~$160B+ and climbing as the build-out runs.

What to watch next

Does FCF inflect?
Net CapEx is guided to ~$70B in FY27, so free cash flow likely stays negative near-term. The turn — whenever it comes — is the whole debate.
Backlog → revenue
How fast $638B of RPO converts into recognized, profitable revenue. Speed of conversion is what justifies the capital.
Financing & dilution
A planned ~$40B raise (incl. $20B equity) and rising debt/lease load test the balance sheet and the investment-grade rating.
